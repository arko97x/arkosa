This website reflects the various explorations that I undertook over the course of five months. The explorations have spanned both the physical and the digital realms. Since their inception, a lot of these works and ideas have encountered several minds on their way to execution. I shall name these co-designers at appropriate junctures. I wish to unpack projects under two broad themes - one being the influence and impact of poetry on the playfulness of an idea/artifact, and the other being designing effective & accessible digital products. As will be evident, poetry introduces an aspect of playfulness to the mix. I wish to, then, address the relationship between play and joy. Two projects under the latter theme are my experiences with trying to understand user behavior encompassing digital products.

To briefly lay out the timeline, all works, and concepts in this document were created at some point during a 5-month period - January through May of 2022. I wish to classify all work under two broad thematic groups; they're titled 'Of Poetry & Play — Building joyful experiences' and 'Of Future Possibilities — Designing effective and accessible digital products'. The first thematic group involves five pieces of work. All of them possess a strand of play in their DNA. Some of them explore the power of language, via poetry, to accentuate the playfulness and thus elicit joy. The second thematic group deals with two projects. They are digital products that have been heavily informed by an understanding of user behaviour and conversations with actual consumers of the product. They highlight my attempt at understanding accessibility and its effect on the future of digital design. It is interesting to note, though, that poetry was something that I noticed while sifting through my body of work. While I’ve always enjoyed the rhythm associated with rhyme, I hadn’t necessarily thought of its ability to elicit emotions. This discovery ties in well with my intial line of inquiry of ‘The Art of Eliciting Emotions’. There are questions that evolved while annotating material on this website, which I intend to bring up in subsequent sections.
